About JijueHealth

Our Detailed Mission

Our mission is to bridge the gap in access to quality SRHR education and services for adolescents and young adults, aged 13–25, living in Kenya's urban areas. We aim to address stigma, shyness, and misinformation by offering innovative solutions that prioritize privacy, inclusivity, and community engagement. Through partnerships with pharmacies, on-ground initiatives, and a digital platform, JijueHealth empowers individuals to make informed decisions about their health and well-being.

Our Detailed Vision

Our vision is to create a future where all adolescents and young adults, regardless of their socio-economic background, can access the information, tools, and support needed to lead healthy, empowered lives. By fostering an environment of inclusivity, trust, and innovation, we envision a society where SRHR is normalized, stigma is eradicated, and young people have the confidence to take charge of their reproductive health.

Our Services

  • SRHR Education: We provide interactive and culturally relevant learning materials through our digital platform and on-ground workshops to increase awareness and knowledge.
  • Access to Services: Partnering with local pharmacies, we offer private and youth-friendly SRHR services, including testing, consultations, and advice.
  • Essential Products: We make SRHR-related products accessible and affordable, including contraceptives, menstrual hygiene products, STI test kits, and fertility monitoring tools.
  • Community Support: Our trained volunteers provide mentorship and guidance tailored to the unique challenges faced by our target audience.
  • Digital Platform: Our online portal connects users with service providers and offers resources for self-learning, ensuring privacy and convenience.

Our Milestones

Since its founding in June 2024, JijueHealth has made significant strides in bridging the gap in Sexual and Reproductive Health and Rights (SRHR) education and services for adolescents and young adults in urban slums. Through our community-centered approach and digital platform, we have successfully:

  • Reached over 500 youth in Kibera through direct engagement, workshops, and online consultations.
  • Conducted 10+ SRHR workshops in collaboration with local community centers, schools, and health organizations.
  • Partnered with 3 local pharmacies, enabling discreet and youth-friendly access to contraceptives and menstrual hygiene products.
  • Distributed over 1,000 SRHR-related products, including condoms, sanitary pads, and emergency contraceptives.
  • Facilitated 100+ one-on-one consultations via our online platform and community health volunteers.
